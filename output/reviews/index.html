VOL. I

ITALIAN POLITICS AS A WORK OF ART

In 2014 Edinburgh Fringe hosted 3,193 shows (source). Someone liked Marcel, someone didn't, but getting 4 reviews in 2 weeks is definitely meaningful.

What I have in mind is that art may be bad, good or indifferent, but, whatever adjective is used, we must call it art, and bad art is still art in the same way that a bad emotion is still an emotion.Marcel Duchamp

Who liked it

The show manages to comment on political corruption, saturation of information, negative representations of women as well as parodying military rigmarole in a generally absurdist fashion.Broadwaybaby
Possibly the most eclectic and sideways commemoration of the First World War at the Fringe. The Scotsman

Who didn't

Striking images of surrealism, but is it art?Festmag
The links between those sequences and the Berlusconi sections are very unclear.Broadwaybaby
Judging by this show, theres unlikely to be a volume two.Threeweeks
The analyses that have been put forward are not necessarily of any value, since I have not offered any explanation myself. [...] What I intended is of no interest; what is interesting is the effect the work has on the spectator. Marcel Duchamp
